Mahatma Gandhi:

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi 2 October 1869 – 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer, [3] anti-colonial nationalist,[4] and political ethicist, [5] who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule, [6] and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā (Sanskrit: "great-souled", "venerable"), first applied to him in 1914 in South Africa, is now used throughout the world.
